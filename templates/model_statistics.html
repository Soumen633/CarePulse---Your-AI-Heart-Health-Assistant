  • Strong Correlations: Systolic and diastolic blood pressure show high correlation (0.72), indicating interconnected cardiovascular metrics
  • BMI Relationships: BMI strongly correlates with weight (0.85), validating our engineered feature

  • Cholesterol-Glucose Interaction: Interaction feature shows correlations of 0.78 with both cholesterol and glucose levels

  • Lifestyle Factors: Smoking and alcohol show moderate correlation (0.34), suggesting lifestyle clustering
  • Dataset Balance: Perfect 50/50 split between disease positive and negative cases ensures unbiased training

Comparative distributions of all features split by cardiovascular disease status (Cardio=0 vs Cardio=1), revealing clear separation patterns in blood pressure, BMI, cholesterol, and other key predictors.

3. Hyperparameter Tuning Impact Analysis

Before vs After Tuning Comparison
Performance improvements across all models after hyperparameter optimization using GridSearchCV and RandomizedSearchCV, showing accuracy and F1-score gains for Decision Tree, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, XGBoost, and CatBoost.
Hyperparameter Tuning Results
  • Decision Tree: Significant improvement (+4.85% accuracy, +2.89% F1) with optimized parameters: criterion='entropy', max_depth=15, min_samples_leaf=2
  • Random Forest: Moderate gain (+2.01% accuracy, +1.28% F1) with pre-tuned configuration
  • Gradient Boosting: Fine-tuned with learning_rate=0.05, max_depth=7, n_estimators=150, subsample=0.9 achieving +0.26% accuracy improvement
  • XGBoost: Best performance with subsample=0.9, n_estimators=300, max_depth=5, learning_rate=0.1, gaining +0.26% accuracy
  • CatBoost: Optimized with depth=4, iterations=300, l2_leaf_reg=1, learning_rate=0.1 for +0.14% accuracy boost
  • Overall Impact: All models benefited from tuning, with tree-based models showing the most dramatic improvements

4. Confusion Matrices - Tuned Models

Prediction Accuracy Breakdown
Detailed confusion matrices showing true positives, true negatives, false positives, and false negatives for all five tuned models after hyperparameter optimization.
Medical Context Considerations

In cardiovascular disease prediction, false negatives (missing a disease case) are typically more critical than false positives. While all models maintain high true negative rates (8163-8275), the balance between precision and recall is crucial for clinical deployment. XGBoost achieves the best balance with highest recall (72.96%) while maintaining strong precision (92.09%).
